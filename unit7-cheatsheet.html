Equations of Change 7.1 – 7.2

Turn a sentence about how something changes into a differential equation.

rate proportional to the amount
proportional to the difference from M
Translating a model
∝ the amount→ \(ky\) (exponential)
∝ difference from M→ \(k(y-M)\); equilibrium at \(y=M\)
constant rate→ \(k\) alone (linear change)
trap
  • Verify by substituting. \(y=f(x)\) is a solution only if both sides match for all \(x\).
  • "Proportional to ___" means \(k\times\) that quantity — never \(+k\).

Slope Fields 7.3

A picture of every solution at once — a tiny segment of slope at each point.

segment slope at a point

Zero slope (horizontal) where \(f=0\); undefined (vertical) where a denominator \(=0\).

Match a field to its DE
same down columns→ depends on \(x\) only
same across rows→ depends on \(y\) only (autonomous)
horizontal here→ set \(f=0\) for the isocline, then test a point's sign
trap
  • Don't swap columns (x-only) and rows (y-only) — always confirm with one test point.

Euler's Method 7.5

Approximate a solution by walking short tangent-line steps.

one step

Recompute the slope at every new point; advance \(x\) by \(h\) each step. Error \(\propto h\) — quarter the step, quarter the error.

Over- or under-estimate?
concave up→ underestimate (tangents below the curve)
concave down→ overestimate (tangents above)
trap
  • Don't reuse the first slope — it changes at each new point.
  • Count the steps to reach the target: \(n=\dfrac{x_{\text{end}}-x_0}{h}\).

Before you solve every problem

A 20-second checklist that catches most lost points.

Always
name the type→ separable? exponential? logistic?
use the IC→ pin the constant before simplifying too far
read the ask→ a value, a time, a rate, or a limit?

Calculator section? Set up the exact equation or integral, then solve numerically — you still earn the setup points even if the algebra is ugly.

Three kinds of growth at a glance

Tell the models apart by what the rate is proportional to.

Rate → shape
constant→ \(y'=k\): a straight line
∝ the amount→ \(y'=ky\): exponential, doubles forever
∝ room left→ \(y'=kP\!\left(1-\tfrac{P}{L}\right)\): logistic S-curve to \(L\)
∝ gap to \(M\)→ \(y'=k(y-M)\): levels off at \(M\)

Spot it fast: a ceiling means logistic or bounded; no ceiling means exponential.

trap
  • Exponential and logistic look identical early — only the long run reveals the ceiling.

Separation of Variables 7.6 – 7.7

All the \(y\)'s on one side, all the \(x\)'s on the other, then integrate.

separate, then integrate both sides

One \(+C\); apply the initial condition, then solve for \(y\). Keep \(|\,\cdot\,|\) in logs — exponentiating turns \(+C\) into \(\times A\).

Is it separable?
a product \(g(x)h(y)\)→ yes, divide by \(h(y)\)
a sum like \(x+y\)→ no, separation fails
trap
  • For \(y^2=\dots\), pick the branch (\(\pm\)) matching the initial sign of \(y\).
  • Domain: the largest interval around \(x_0\) where the solution stays defined.

Exponential Growth & Decay 7.8

When the rate is proportional to the amount, change is exponential.

solve \(y'=ky\)
half-life & doubling time

Relative rate \(\tfrac{y'}{y}=k\) is constant. Solve for \(t\): isolate \(e^{kt}\), take \(\ln\), divide by \(k\).

trap
  • Exponential growth multiplies by a fixed factor per interval — it is not linear \((y_0+kt)\).

Logistic Model 7.9

Growth that speeds up, then levels off at a carrying capacity — the S-curve.

logistic differential equation
explicit solution

Capacity \(L\); equilibria \(P=0,\ P=L\). Fastest at \(P=\tfrac{L}{2}\), max rate \(\tfrac{kL}{4}\). As \(t\to\infty\), \(P\to L\) and \(\tfrac{dP}{dt}\to 0\).

Reading a logistic problem
the ceiling→ \(L\) (factor \(kP(1-P/L)\))
fastest growth→ \(P=L/2\)
value at time \(t\)→ build \(P(t)\), \(A=\tfrac{L-P_0}{P_0}\)
trap
  • Solutions inflect at \(L/2\) — not concave up throughout.
  • Early on it mimics exponential, but bends toward \(L\) — don't project it straight up.

AP-style asks on the exam

What the question is really asking — and the move that answers it.

The ask → the move
value at time \(t\)→ plug \(t\) into the solution \(y(t)\)
when it reaches \(X\)→ set \(y(t)=X\), solve for \(t\) with \(\ln\)
average value→ \(\dfrac{1}{b-a}\displaystyle\int_a^b y\,dt\)
rate right now→ read \(\dfrac{dy}{dt}\) straight from the DE
trap
  • Don't re-solve for an instantaneous rate — the DE already gives it.
∂

All of Unit 7 is one idea: a differential equation states the rule of change — solving it, or just reading its slope field, turns that rule into the actual function. Growth, decay, motion, populations: same move every time. Name the model, use the condition, answer the ask.
